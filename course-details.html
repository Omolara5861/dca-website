Agile Project Management Program

An Agile Product Manager (also called an APM) plans, leads, organizes and motivates Agile project teams. The APM has a vast range of responsibilities: Helping the team achieve a high level of performance and quality, holding teams accountable for their work, removing obstacles, and mentoring less experienced team members. Project managers manage the scope, schedule, and budget of a project while also providing the leadership and controls necessary to enable business change.

Course Overview

Our APM practical training empowers candidates to lead project teams. Candidates will simulate scrum ceremonies weekly including sprint planning, daily stand-up, sprint review and sprint retrospective as practiced within Agile environments.

The program is modelled after the most recent PMP curriculum to prepare,candidates with skills to lead projects using waterfall and agile methodologies. Project management is in high demand across all industries,worldwide, and it can lead to executive positions.

Agile Scrum Ceremonies:

Sprint Planning:

This is a meeting for the team to consider items of work to be completed and commits to what they have capacity to deliver during the coming Sprint.

Daily Stand-Up:

This is a 15-minute team meeting to provide updates and progress of individual work done towards adding value to product development.

Scrum Review:

This is where the team showcases what they have completed for the Stakeholders’ review and feedback and get a sign-off.

Scrum Retrospective:

This is a meeting where the team reflects on their performance during the current Sprint and seek ways to improve their interaction, practices and lessons learnt

Sprint:

This is a cycle of work for the team (can be 1 - 4 weeks interval)
